A- Réaction à un message écrit

Contexte: Dans la tradition africaine, la dot est un prix capital à payer avant l'union d'un homme et d'une femme et de leurs familles respectives.

Text: About Dowry

1- A dowry is a transfer of parental property at the marriage of a daughter. Dowry contrasts with the concepts of bride price and dower. While bride price is a payment by the groom or his family to the bride's parents, dowry is the wealth transferred from the bride's family to the groom or his family. Similarly, dower is the property settled on the bride herself by the groom at the time of marriage.

2- Dowry is an ancient custom. But it continues to be expected, and demanded as a condition to accept a marriage proposal in some parts of the world. Disputes related to dowry sometimes result in acts of violence against women, including killings and acid attacks.

3- In Egypt, a dowry is known as Gehaz. This is the property a bride is expected to bring with her at marriage, and it is different from the dower paid by the groom to the bride. Gehaz is observed in rural Egypt and is typically negotiated between the groom's family and the bride's. Gehaz includes furniture, appliances, jewelry, bedding and various household items. Families begin collecting dowry years before a girl is betrothed. Many Egyptian girls take up jobs so as to save money necessary to meet the expected dowry demands.

4- Every piece of the Gehaz is placed on open cars that go around the village several times, with music in order to show off the dowry being given by the bride family to the groom.

Adapted from www.wikipediacommons/dowry.com
Vocabulary notes

1. bride: la mariée

2. groom: le marié

3. to betroth: se fiancer

Item 1: Answer by True or False

1 Dower and dowry mean the same thing.
False Correct Answer: False

Correction

Answer: False

Justification: According to paragraph 1, "dowry is the wealth transferred from the bride's family to the groom or his family" while "dower is the property settled on the bride herself by the groom at the time of marriage." They are different concepts.

2 Dowry is no longer paid nowadays.
False Correct Answer: False

Correction

Answer: False

Justification: Paragraph 2 states that "it continues to be expected, and demanded as a condition to accept a marriage proposal in some parts of the world."

3 Dower is a transfer of property of the bride to the groom.
False Correct Answer: False

Correction

Answer: False

Justification: According to paragraph 1, "dower is the property settled on the bride herself by the groom" - it's from the groom to the bride, not from the bride to the groom.

4 Gehaz includes items worn as ornaments.
True Correct Answer: True

Correction

Answer: True

Justification: Paragraph 3 mentions that "Gehaz includes furniture, appliances, jewelry, bedding and various household items." Jewelry are items worn as ornaments.

Item 2: Answer these questions on the text

1 What is the difference between dower and dowry?

Correction

Answer: Dowry is the wealth transferred from the bride's family to the groom or his family, while dower is the property settled on the bride herself by the groom at the time of marriage.

Reference: Paragraph 1 explains this distinction clearly.

2 Is dowry an old custom? Justify your answer through a sentence from the text.

Correction

Answer: Yes, dowry is an old custom.

Justification: The text states in paragraph 2: "Dowry is an ancient custom."

3 How do Egyptian girls prepare for Gehaz?

Correction

Answer: Many Egyptian girls take up jobs to save money necessary to meet the expected dowry demands.

Reference: This information is found in paragraph 3 of the text.

4 Why are pieces displayed on open cars during Gehaz in Egypt?

Correction

Answer: The pieces are displayed on open cars to show off the dowry being given by the bride's family to the groom.

Reference: Paragraph 4 explains this practice.

Item 3: Choose the right answer from the brackets

1 Tradition ( ought, oughtn't, should) be respected for the harmony of African society

Correction

Answer: should

Correct sentence: Tradition should be respected for the harmony of African society.

Explanation: "Should" is used to express recommendation or moral obligation.

2 They phoned the bride's parents (so, for, in order) to agree on the date of the wedding.

Correction

Answer: to

Correct sentence: They phoned the bride's parents to agree on the date of the wedding.

Explanation: "To" is used to express purpose. Note: "in order to" would also be correct but is not among the options.

3 The spouses ( were praying, was praying, played) when we came

Correction

Answer: were praying

Correct sentence: The spouses were praying when we came.

Explanation: "Were praying" is the past continuous tense, used for an action in progress when another action happened.

4 This is a ( dangerous, danger, dangerous) marriage because the groom is an AIDS carrier

Correction

Answer: dangerous

Correct sentence: This is a dangerous marriage because the groom is an AIDS carrier.

Explanation: "Dangerous" is the adjective form needed to describe the noun "marriage."

5 The bride is 50 years old; she can ………wear a dress ……a skirt. ( neither…nor, either…or, either…nor)

Correction

Answer: either…or

Correct sentence: The bride is 50 years old; she can either wear a dress or a skirt.

Explanation: "Either…or" is used to present alternatives or choices.

6 Only (few, a few, little ) guests come to the marriage

Correction

Answer: a few

Correct sentence: Only a few guests come to the marriage.

Explanation: "A few" is used with countable nouns (guests) to mean a small number.

Item 4: Rephrase these sentences. Use the given prompts

1 If the groom were not an AIDS carrier, we would accept the dowry

- Weren't......

Correction

Answer: Weren't the groom an AIDS carrier, we would accept the dowry.

Explanation: This is an inverted conditional structure using "were" in place of "if".

2 Adeline received presents from her in laws.

- Presents ......

Correction

Answer: Presents were received by Adeline from her in laws.

Explanation: This is the passive voice transformation of the original active sentence.

3 People used this practice extensively.

- This practice......

Correction

Answer: This practice was used extensively.

Explanation: This is another passive voice transformation.

4 "These negotiations can take up to two days," said the author.

- The author said......

Correction

Answer: The author said that those negotiations could take up to two days.

Explanation: This is reported speech with the necessary changes (these→those, can→could).

II - Production d'un texte de type particulier

Contexte : Le mariage traditionnel est important pour la validité du mariage africain.

Critère d'évaluation : Tu montreras ta capacité à produire un texte cohérent en rédigeant un paragraphe cohérent, respectant la logique interne des idées et utilisant le vocabulaire et la grammaire appropriés.

Writing Complete this paragraph with these words: traditional – relatives – groom – bride – dowry.

The price paid to take a wife is called……1……. The girl who receives the price is the……2……and the man who paid the price is the……3……The people who are sent for the dowry to be given are called……4…….Dowry giving is a……5……..ceremony.

NB: Don't copy the paragraph (write only the number et the suitable words)

Correction

Completed paragraph:

The price paid to take a wife is called dowry. The girl who receives the price is the bride and the man who paid the price is the groom. The people who are sent for the dowry to be given are called relatives. Dowry giving is a traditional ceremony.

Answer format as requested:

1. dowry

2. bride

3. groom

4. relatives

5. traditional
